Extreme Heat Forces South Sudan to Close Schools

Description: Extreme heat waves in South Sudan, exacerbated by climate change, have compelled the closure of schools in the nation. Forecasted temperatures exceeding 113 degrees Fahrenheit, unusual for the dry season, prompted authorities to shut down schools for at least two weeks. The lack of infrastructure, such as air-conditioning, exacerbates the challenge for schools, particularly outside the capital, Juba. This closure not only disrupts the academic calendar but also exacerbates existing challenges in a country already grappling with conflict, displacement, and food insecurity.

South Sudan Shuts Down All Schools Ahead of Heatwave

Description: In response to an impending extreme heat wave, South Sudan's government has taken proactive measures, closing all schools for two weeks to protect children from soaring temperatures reaching up to 45 degrees Celsius (113 Fahrenheit). Health and education ministries urged parents to keep children indoors during this period. The decision aims to safeguard against health risks associated with the heat. Peter Garang, a resident of Juba, supports the move, suggesting schools be equipped with electricity for air conditioning. South Sudan faces numerous challenges, including climate vulnerability exacerbated by civil conflict, droughts, and floods, underscoring the importance of proactive measures for public safety and well-being.

South Sudan Closes Schools Due to Extreme Heat

Description: In response to soaring temperatures reaching between 41 to 45 degrees Celsius, South Sudan has declared an indefinite closure of all schools starting March 18. Health Minister Yolanda Awel Deng emphasized the severity of the heatwave, warning of potential health risks including heat exhaustion and stroke. With cases of heat-related deaths already reported, the government urges parents to monitor children closely. Environmental officials highlight the broader impact of climate change, urging global emission reductions. The move underscores the urgent need for adaptation measures to mitigate the growing threat of extreme weather events in the region.
